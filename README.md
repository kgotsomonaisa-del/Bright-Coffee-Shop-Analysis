Case Objective
BrightTV's CEO wants insights that help the CVM team grow the subscription base. The case study specifically asks for:

User and usage trends
Factors that influence consumption
Content recommendations for low-consumption days
Initiatives to grow the user base
Source Files Used
User Profiles.xlsx
Viewership.xlsx
BrightTV_Case_Study.pdf
All source timestamps were interpreted as UTC and converted to South African time (Africa/Johannesburg) during analysis.

Key Analytical Highlights
4,455 user profiles were supplied, and 10,000 viewing sessions were analyzed.
4,386 unique viewers appear in viewership data, with 4,211 matching a known user profile.
Total observed consumption is 91,342.32 minutes.
Average session duration is 9.14 minutes, and median session duration is 1.80 minutes, indicating a long-tail pattern with many short sessions and a smaller set of high-duration sessions.
Monday is the weakest day for consumption after converting UTC timestamps into South African time.
Prime viewing is concentrated between 16:00 and 22:00 SA time, with the strongest hour at 18:00.
Gauteng and Western Cape drive the largest share of total minutes.
Adults aged 25-34 and 35-44 account for the largest concentration of viewership minutes.
The strongest channels by total watch time are ICC Cricket World Cup 2011, Supersport Live Events, Channel O, and Trace TV.
Recommendation Direction
Use sports tentpoles and music-led content to lift weak early-week consumption, especially Monday and Tuesday.
Focus acquisition and retention campaigns on Gauteng and Western Cape, then extend into KwaZulu-Natal and Mpumalanga.
Prioritize the 25-44 age bands in growth campaigns because they combine the largest audience pool with strong engagement.
Build day-parted campaigns around the 16:00-22:00 peak window.
